Feelings after Reading the First Four Books of Magic Tree House

Posted on 2024-09-26

Background

Having learned English passively for many years in preparation for exams, I still feel my proficiency is quite limited. Recently, I have made a conscious effort to improve, and I would like to share some of my experiences and feelings here.

Feelings

I came across the Magic Tree House series through a helpful video recommendation. Excited by how useful it seemed, I immediately placed an order online. After eagerly awaiting their arrival, I received the first four books of the series and started reading them right away. It took me about two weeks to finish, which felt rewarding. Here are my feelings:

1. So many pretty common verbs and nouns that I do not know at all

There were many common verbs and nouns that are used in everyday life, but I had never learned them. For example, nouns like oak tree, rope ladder, bush, and porch, and verbs like peer, crawl, tiptoe, and frown—these are simple, everyday words, yet I was unfamiliar with their meanings, which meant I had to spend a lot of time looking them up while reading.

I guess the reason is that I used to learn English from textbooks written by Chinese teachers, many of whom had likely never been abroad. The content was far removed from authentic, natural English. To make matters worse, I spent so much time doing drills just to get good grades on exams, which meant what I learned was not even proper, everyday English. On top of that, I rarely used English in my daily life. It could not have been worse!
